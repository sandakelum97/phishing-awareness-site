What is Phishing?

Phishing is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details by disguising as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication, typically an email. Attackers use these emails to distribute malicious links or attachments that can steal credentials or install malware.

Key Signs of Phishing

  • Sense of Urgency: Language that urges you to act immediately.
  • Suspicious Sender: The sender's email address looks odd or is a variation of a known address.
  • Strange Links: Hover over links to see the actual destination URL before clicking.
  • Spelling & Grammar Errors: Professional companies usually proofread their emails.
  • Unexpected Attachments: Be wary of attachments you weren't expecting.
  • Generic Greetings: Phishing emails often use greetings like "Dear Valued Customer."
